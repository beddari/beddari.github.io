#cfgmgmtcamp 2019 monday schedule tips

Feb 4, 2019
3 min read

Originally cfgmgmtcamp was a single track conference and although aspects of that still remains in 2019 the organizers have taken a liberal approach to adding more tracks. This is awesome but it really makes it challenging to get an overview of what is happening.

The amount of relevant content available is astounding and knowing that not all of them will be recorded leads to my first tip.

Do not stay where you think you belong!

This is the most important tip. Outside of the main track, if you are a tool user, try and limit the time you spend at $HOME. Be curious, and invest time in other speakers and tracks than what you first think is the most relevant.

Another clue here is to try and find the speakers that take a generic approach to solving problems in their talks. These are the ones you’d like to spend your time with.

Skip the late parallell talks in the main rooms if they are recorded

I know, not fair versus those speaking there, but I’m first and foremost a pragmatic and if I can watch one talk later as a recording then I won’t be selecting to attend that versus missing out on other non-recorded speakers.

Be aware that my impression is that recording isn’t a high pri concern for the organizers. They seem to knowingly focus first on the speakers and attendes on site. If you follow my advice and later find that a recording is missing or bad, you have been warned!

14:00 Ansible community update

It has taken a good while but finally Ansible is getting a real community of its own. And much of that can be attributed to Robyn’s work! If you are a user this talk would be essential.

15:00 Solving large scale operational metadata problems using stream processing

When RI speaks, I listen. This should be focused on practical issues and problem solving. An inside look at managing large heterogenous fleets of enterprise software with modern tooling? Can’t wait.

17:30 Drilling down a software bug

Interesting speaker and a “story mode” topic. I’ll take a chance on this one =)

Avoid the “social event”

The cfgmtmtcamp social event for me is perhaps the weakest part of the conference. Sadly, arriving at the place mostly breaks up any interesting convo you’d be having on the way over there.

Perhaps this is a problem with the location, but every year I go there I always just want to leave for dinner to have proper conversations. I haven’t publicly tried to comment on this before, but I feel this is a missed opportunity. Long timers will have several unofficial invitations and events to choose from but that just feels counter-intuivite to the purpose of the conference. Be open, be inclusive. We need a better way to do this.

My advice would be to ask around to join one of the private events or just head straight for the city center for food with hopefully interesting people.
